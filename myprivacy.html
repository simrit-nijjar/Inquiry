Goal 1: HOW MUCH ACCESS TO MY INFO DO I GIVE TO DIFFERENT COMPANIES?

Google:

1. Personalization:
  • Web & App Activity: Keeps track of your web and app activity.
  • Location History: Keeps records of your location history
  • Device Information: Keeps tab of the type and model of your device.
  • Voice and Audio Activity: Keeps tabs of your voice and sends it to Google.
  • Youtube Search History
  • Youtube Watch History

2. Youtube:
  • Likes and Subscriptions: Google has access to all your ratings and subscriptions.
  • Activity Feed: Google has access to all your subscriptions and playlists.

3. Google Photos:
  • Faces: Gives Google the face of those in your Google Photo Drive
  • Photo Geolocation: Gives google access to all locations of photos you upload

4. Google+
  • Community Posts
  • Photos and Videos: Gives access to google to use your photos for their products.

5. Ad Personalization:
  • Ad Personalization: Keeps track your age and demographic to serve you ads.

6. General Information:
  • Birthday
  • Phone Number
  • Gender
  • Emails

Microsoft:

Information was taked directly from Microsoft's page
1. Browsing History:
If browsing history in Cortana is turned on, your Microsoft Edge browsing history is sent to Microsoft so that Microsoft features and services may use this data to provide you with timely and intelligent answers, proactive personalized suggestions, or to complete tasks for you.

2. Search History:
Like other search engines, Bing uses your search history to give you better results, including personalization and autosuggest. Cortana also uses that data to give you timely, intelligent answers, personalized suggestions, and complete other tasks for you.

3. Location Activity:
To give you directions to the places you want to go, and show you data relevant to where you are, we use locations that you provide or that we've detected using technologies like GPS.

4. Voice Activity:
When you use voice commands with Windows, Cortana, and other cloud-based, voice-enabled products and services from Microsoft, we will collect and store your audio recordings so that we can enhance your experience with better speech recognition and other personalized speech experiences. Microsoft uses your voice command data to improve the ability of its products and services to correctly recognize your pronunciation and speech patterns.

5. Media Activity:
When you watch movies or TV on a Microsoft app or service, we collect data about your media activity so that we can provide you with more relevant recommendations for entertainment and content.

6. Product and Service Activity:
When you use a Microsoft product or service, we may save data about your activity to your Microsoft account – such as info about which apps or services you use and how you use them. This info helps improve our products and make them work better for you.

7. Product and Service Performance:
When you use a Microsoft product or service, we collect reliability and performance data to measure the quality of your experience. This data tells us about the health of these products and services and helps us fix and improve them.

8. Cortana's Notebook:
To help you avoid traffic, remember anniversaries, text the right “Jennifer” in your contact list, and in general do more, Cortana needs to know what you're interested in, what's on your calendar, and who you might want to do things with. The Notebook is where Cortana keeps track of your interests to give you a personalized experience. When you don't want to reach for a keyboard, Cortana can use your speech and to help translate what you say or write into documents and text messages.

9. LinkedIn:
When you connect your Microsoft and LinkedIn accounts, Microsoft shares some of your account data with LinkedIn, such as calendar and contact data. Likewise, LinkedIn shares some of your account data with Microsoft, such as profile and connections data.

10. Health Activity:
Microsoft Health, HealthVault, and devices like Microsoft Band can help you collect, understand, and manage your health data. Your data can include activity and fitness data like heart rate and daily steps taken. It can also include any health records you store in HealthVault. And HealthVault gives you the ability to share health records with caregivers.

Apple:

Personal Information

1. Name:
The name used to set up your Apple ID is stored with Apple.

2. Address:
The address provided when first creating your Apple ID is stored within Apple's servers.

3. Email:
The Email used to create your Apple ID is stored with Apple. They also have the iCloud email that they provide you when first creating your Apple ID.

4. Phone Number:
When first creating your Apple ID, the number used with your account is stored with Apple. Your device number is also most likely stored with Apple.

5. Device Identifiers:
They keep track of your device type, model, serial number, etc

6. IP Address:
They have your Internet Service Provider, Mac Address, IPV4 Address, IPV6 Address, etc.

7. Location Information:
Your device's location is usually sent to Apple, but only if you enable it when first using Apple Maps.

8. Credit Card Information:
The credit card that you associate with your Apple ID is saved to Apple Servers. It is, however, encrypted to only be seen with access to your Apple ID account.

Non-Personal Information

1. Occupation:
Your current job description, if you've given it to Apple at any time.

2. Language:
The language you set on your device as default.

3. Zip Code:
A code used to improve the postal service delivery service. Used by Apple to deliver products bought from Apple.

4. Area Code:
The three numbers used at the front of your phone number.

5. Device Identifiers:
They keep track of your device type, model, serial number, etc

6. Location:
The location of your device if allowed by the user at first. Also includes the location used for delivery when used to order from Apple.

7. Time Zone:
The time zone set by you or your device device when set to automatic using your location.
